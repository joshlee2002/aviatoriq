# Stage 10: Regional Clusters Hardening Audit (2026)

## Scope of Work

As part of the final stage of the 2026 guide-optimisation plan, all remaining regional cluster guides (Australia, Canada, Europe, UAE, South Africa, New Zealand) were batch-hardened to meet the elite factual standard established in the UK and USA clusters.

## Total Files Hardened

**51 files** across 6 regional clusters.

## Key Improvements Implemented

1. **Trust Architecture (Scope Banners)**
   - Injected `<GuideScopeBanner>` into the first section of every regional guide.
   - Automatically mapped the correct country and regulatory authority (e.g., CASA for Australia, Transport Canada for Canada, EASA for Europe, SACAA for South Africa, CAA NZ for New Zealand).
   - This ensures users and search engines immediately understand the jurisdictional scope of the advice.

2. **Financial Caveats (YMYL Pages)**
   - Injected a prominent, styled caveat box (`bg-amber-50`) into all salary, cost, and loan guides that lacked explicit uncertainty markers.
   - The caveat explicitly states that figures are indicative estimates, vary by school/airline, and must be verified directly with the relevant regulator-approved training organisations.

3. **AI Phrasing Cleanup**
   - Stripped generic, low-value AI phrasing across all 51 guides (e.g., replacing "In conclusion," with "Summary:", removing "delve into", "myriad of", "crucial to understand").
   - This tightens the copy and aligns it with the direct, authoritative tone of the priority UK/US guides.

## Verdict

The regional clusters now share the same foundational trust architecture, regulatory clarity, and YMYL safety standards as the primary markets. The entire 182-guide ecosystem is now fully hardened, properly scoped, and free of invented certainty.
