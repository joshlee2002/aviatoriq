## Executive verdict

These ten guides are now **closer to publishable for general informational use**, but not “perfect” in the absolute sense because several topics depend on live airline recruitment pages, lender terms, CAA policy interpretation, or individual medical/financial circumstances. The second pass removed the highest-risk overclaims: guaranteed-job language, speculative self-funded type-rating advice, rigid medical disqualifier wording, outdated RAF age language, and overconfident pilot-shortage/salary claims.

## Official/current-source checks used

This batch was checked against the same 2026 research standard used for the first UK batch, prioritising primary or highly credible sources:

- UK CAA licensing and medical guidance, including Class 1 medical context, AME/aeromedical decision-making, CPL/ATPL and exam-rule context.
- Airline/cadet programme pages where claims are programme-specific, including British Airways Speedbird, Jet2 FlightPath and other UK/European cadet-route pages.
- Student-finance and UK personal-finance principles for claims about loans, LISAs and training finance.
- RAF recruitment pages for pilot age limits and selection/training-path constraints.
- Industry salary/shortage context treated as directional, not guaranteed, because airline hiring and pay move quickly.

## Remaining risks before “number 1 on Google” standard

1. **Expert review:** Medical and finance content needs named reviewer input or an editorial-review note to fully satisfy YMYL trust expectations.
2. **Live programme freshness:** Cadet programme pages should have a recurring update workflow because places, funding and eligibility change frequently.
3. **Original value:** To compete for top rankings, add proprietary extras: UK training-cost calculator, downloadable finance checklist, cadet-programme comparison table, RAF-vs-civilian decision worksheet and interview-prep templates.
4. **Source transparency:** Each page should ideally show “last checked” plus exact sources in the visible article footer, not just generic source fallbacks.
5. **SERP intent matching:** Some pages still need stronger above-the-fold answer boxes: exact cost, timeline, eligibility, risks and next step.

## Bottom line

The second ten are **not rushed and materially improved**. I would publish them as useful informational guides after this pass, with two caveats: medical/finance pages should be labelled informational rather than personalised advice, and any page relying on live airline or RAF recruitment data must be rechecked on a fixed editorial schedule.
